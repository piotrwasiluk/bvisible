## **30-second elevator pitch**

**bVisible** is an AI visibility command center for in-house SEO teams.

Teams enter their site, competitors, and focus areas. bVisible then shows which high-intent prompts matter, where the brand appears in AI answers, what content gets cited, where competitors lead, and what to publish next.

It should feel like **Linear for AI visibility**: minimal, sharp, fast, monochrome, and built for execution.

## **Problem & mission**

### **Problem**

SEO teams can see search rankings. They cannot clearly see how AI models represent their brand.

Today, that creates five problems:

* **Prompt blindness:** Teams do not know which prompts shape discovery.  
* **Visibility ambiguity:** Teams cannot easily tell where their brand appears, ranks, or gets omitted.  
* **Citation opacity:** Teams do not know which pages earn citations from AI answers.  
* **Competitive drift:** Competitors may own key topics before anyone notices.  
* **Action paralysis:** Raw monitoring data rarely turns into a clear publishing backlog.

### **Mission**

Help in-house SEO teams understand and improve how AI models see, cite, and compare their brand.

### **Product promise**

bVisible turns AI visibility from a vague concern into an operational workflow:

* Measure visibility  
* Understand why gaps exist  
* Prioritize the next content move  
* Recheck progress over time

## **Conceptual data model**

Think of the system as a workspace with recurring audits.

### **Core entities**

* **User**  
  * Belongs to one or more workspaces  
* **Workspace**  
  * Holds team settings, subscription, and shared data  
* **Website**  
  * The primary brand site being tracked  
* **Competitor**  
  * A tracked competing domain within a workspace  
* **Prompt Cluster**  
  * A grouped set of prompts by intent type  
* **Prompt**  
  * A single query checked across models  
* **Model Check**  
  * A run of a prompt against a specific model at a point in time  
* **Answer Result**  
  * The observed answer output and brand/competitor presence  
* **Citation**  
  * A cited URL, domain, page type, and topic relationship  
* **Topic Gap**  
  * A topic where competitors outperform the brand  
* **Recommendation**  
  * A proposed action linked to a topic, page, or gap  
* **Report**  
  * A snapshot export for stakeholders  
* **Plan / Subscription**  
  * Billing and usage state

### **ERD sketch in words**

* A **workspace** has many **users**, **websites**, **competitors**, and **reports**  
* A **website** has many **prompt clusters**  
* A **prompt cluster** has many **prompts**  
* A **prompt** has many **model checks**  
* A **model check** produces one or more **answer results** and **citations**  
* **Citations** and **answer results** roll up into **visibility metrics**  
* **Topic gaps** are derived from prompt and competitor outcomes  
* **Recommendations** are created from topic gaps, weak pages, and citation patterns

## **UI design principles**

### **1\) State first**

Users should understand the situation immediately.

Example:

* Better: **Missing in comparison prompts**  
* Worse: a decorative chart that needs explanation

### **2\) Delta second**

After status, show change.

Example:

* Visibility score down  
* Citation rate rising  
* Competitor gaining in one topic cluster

### **3\) Explanation third**

Once the user sees the issue, explain why it matters.

### **4\) Quiet by default**

* Monochrome base  
* Semantic color only for meaning  
* Minimal chrome  
* Dense information with calm spacing

### **5\) Scan before analyze**

Follow Steve Krug’s rule: users should not have to think hard to orient themselves.

Use:

* Clear table labels  
* Compact summaries  
* Strong hierarchy  
* Obvious filters  
* Simple winning / tied / losing language

### **6\) Fast triage over deep exploration**

The interface should help users answer:

* Where are we weak?  
* Why?  
* What should we do next?

### **7\) Keyboard-friendly workflow**

Support operator behavior:

* Quick switching  
* Fast filtering  
* Clear focus states  
* Workspace momentum

### **8\) Kind through clarity**

The product should feel intelligent without feeling cold.

That means:

* Calm copy  
* Helpful empty states  
* Restrained motion  
* Clear next steps

## **Risks & mitigations**

### **Risk: unclear trust in AI visibility metrics**

Why it matters:

* If teams do not trust the numbers, they will not act

Mitigation:

* Show methodology clearly  
* Use plain-language explanations  
* Pair scores with concrete evidence:  
  * prompts  
  * citations  
  * examples  
  * competitor comparisons

### **Risk: too much dashboard noise**

Why it matters:

* SEO teams need signal fast

Mitigation:

* Prioritize state, delta, and next move  
* Hide low-value detail by default  
* Use compact summaries over decorative charts

### **Risk: recommendations feel generic**

Why it matters:

* Generic advice kills credibility

Mitigation:

* Tie every recommendation to:  
  * a topic gap  
  * a prompt pattern  
  * a citation weakness  
  * a competitor advantage

### **Risk: onboarding feels heavy**

Why it matters:

* Users may drop before first value

Mitigation:

* Keep setup short  
* Use progressive disclosure  
* Make the intake feel like workspace configuration

### **Risk: product drifts toward “reporting only”**

Why it matters:

* Reporting tools are easier to ignore

Mitigation:

* Treat recommendations as a real work queue  
* Track action status and recheck cycles  
* Build toward recurring operational use

### **Risk: design becomes sterile**

Why it matters:

* Monochrome products can feel cold or empty

Mitigation:

* Use hierarchy, spacing, and motion to add warmth  
* Keep microcopy calm and competent  
* Make the interface feel quietly supportive
